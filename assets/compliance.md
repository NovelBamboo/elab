---

## 1) What a new law actually creates

When a law drops, it doesn’t just create “compliance.”

It creates **four markets**:

### A. Interpretation (high value, low supply)

- What does this actually mean?
    
- Who is affected?
    
- What’s vague or exploitable?
    

**Buyers:** execs, investors, product teams  
**Providers:** top law firms, policy analysts

---

### B. Strategy (highest leverage)

- Should we enter/exit a market?
    
- Can we pivot our product to benefit?
    
- Are there grants, incentives, loopholes?
    

**Buyers:** founders, VCs, operators  
**Providers:** almost nobody systematically

---

### C. Implementation (your “compliance consulting”)

- What do we have to do to not get fined?
    
- Policies, documentation, audits
    

**Buyers:** everyone forced to comply  
**Providers:** crowded field (consultants, SaaS tools)

---

### D. Enforcement / tooling (scale play)

- Software to automate compliance
    
- Monitoring, reporting, alerts
    

**Buyers:** mid-large companies  
**Providers:** RegTech startups

---

## 2) Why “compliance” is what you keep seeing

Because:

- It’s **mandatory demand** (people _must_ buy it)
    
- It’s **easy to explain**
    
- It’s **sellable to small businesses**
    

But:

- It’s reactive
    
- It’s commoditizing fast (AI will eat a lot of it)
    
- It rarely creates outsized leverage
    

---

## 3) The blind spot you’re noticing (this is the real play)

You’re not actually interested in compliance.

You’re noticing:

> Laws reshape markets → nobody systematically translates that into **opportunity maps**

That’s a different category:

- “This law exists” → commodity
    
- “Here’s how to comply” → service
    
- **“Here’s how to profit from it before others notice” → leverage**

## 4) Where your idea actually fits

What you described is closer to:

- **Regulatory intelligence + opportunity engine**
    
- **Policy arbitrage system**
    
- **Legislation-to-business pipeline**
    

Example:

New environmental regulation drops:

That’s upstream of compliance.
