## ANALYZE

- Journeys must show **real behavior + system response**
    
- Must end in a **clear “win condition”**
    
- Must reflect **decision-making, not browsing**
    

👉 System is a **decision engine**, so each journey must end in:

> **Action taken, not insight gained**

---

## 🛤 3 User Journeys

---

# 🛤 1. Operator Journey (Primary Candidate)

### Stage 1: Trigger (Policy Drop / Market Shift)

- **Goal:** Find new business opportunities
    
- **Action:** Opens platform after hearing about a new law or funding change
    
- **System Response:** Shows:
    
    - top impacted sectors
        
    - where money is flowing
        
    - urgency level
        

---

### Stage 2: Opportunity Discovery

- **Goal:** Identify something worth building or pivoting into
    
- **Action:** Scans ranked opportunities
    
- **System Response:** Displays:
    
    - “Top 5 opportunities”
        
    - expected demand
        
    - dependencies (grants, compliance, location)
        

---

### Stage 3: Validation

- **Goal:** Decide if this is real or hype
    
- **Action:** Clicks into one opportunity
    
- **System Response:** Provides:
    
    - policy → funding → demand trace
        
    - confidence score
        
    - risks + constraints
        

---

### Stage 4: Execution Decision (WIN)

- **Goal:** Take action
    
- **Action:** Chooses to:
    
    - build
        
    - pivot
        
    - or partner
        
- **System Response:** Gives:
    
    - step-by-step execution path
        
    - first actions to take within 7 days
        

👉 **Win Condition:**  
User commits to a real-world action (build, hire, partner)

---

---

# 🛤 2. Capital Allocator Journey

### Stage 1: Signal Detection

- **Goal:** Find early investment signals
    
- **Action:** Logs in daily / checks alerts
    
- **System Response:** Highlights:
    
    - new policy-driven capital flows
        
    - sectors gaining funding
        

---

### Stage 2: Opportunity Filtering

- **Goal:** Narrow to high-upside opportunities
    
- **Action:** Filters by:
    
    - sector
        
    - capital intensity
        
    - timeline
        
- **System Response:** Ranks:
    
    - opportunities by expected return
        
    - speed of market reaction
        

---

### Stage 3: Risk & Upside Assessment

- **Goal:** Justify investment decision
    
- **Action:** Reviews details
    
- **System Response:** Shows:
    
    - projected growth
        
    - risks (policy reversal, legal friction)
        
    - comparable past cases
        

---

### Stage 4: Capital Deployment (WIN)

- **Goal:** Allocate capital early
    
- **Action:** Invests, backs a founder, or reallocates portfolio
    
- **System Response:** Provides:
    
    - timing guidance
        
    - positioning insight
        

👉 **Win Condition:**  
Capital is deployed before broader market consensus

---

---

# 🛤 3. Policy Strategist Journey

### Stage 1: Policy Analysis Trigger

- **Goal:** Understand real-world impact of a policy
    
- **Action:** Inputs or selects a policy
    
- **System Response:** Breaks it down into:
    
    - structural changes
        
    - funding shifts
        

---

### Stage 2: Translation to Economic Impact

- **Goal:** Explain impact to stakeholders
    
- **Action:** Reviews system outputs
    
- **System Response:** Maps:
    
    - policy → sectors
        
    - winners / losers
        
    - second-order effects
        

---

### Stage 3: Insight Packaging

- **Goal:** Communicate clearly
    
- **Action:** Uses outputs for reports / briefings
    
- **System Response:** Provides:
    
    - structured summaries
        
    - traceable logic
        
    - source links
        

---

### Stage 4: Advisory Output (WIN)

- **Goal:** Influence decisions
    
- **Action:** Advises leadership, clients, or orgs
    
- **System Response:** Supports:
    
    - defensible recommendations
        

👉 **Win Condition:**  
User successfully explains and influences a real decision

## CHALLENGE (Where your product could break)

1. **Operator journey is strongest → likely your wedge**
    
2. Allocator journey requires:
    
    - stronger **quantification**
        
    - otherwise feels too qualitative
        
3. Strategist journey:
    
    - valuable, but may not drive revenue
        

👉 Biggest risk:

> Trying to serve all 3 equally → weak product
