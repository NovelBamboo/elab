## Round 0 — Orchestration

**Artifact Declaration**  
A short strategic non-fiction memo for ELaB / Every Law a Business.

**Audience & Use Context**  
Entrepreneurs, investors, and operators deciding whether ELaB is worth building, funding, or subscribing to.

**Claim Contract**  
ELaB is not legal news. It is regulatory opportunity intelligence: a system that converts government action into business opportunity maps. Source basis: ELaB positioning and product notes.

**Intent & Effect**  
Make the reader understand the wedge, trust problem, and first build path without hype.

**Validation Frameworks**  
Use only as internal checks: claim clarity, evidence sufficiency, commercial specificity, trust risk. No framework language may appear in artifact prose, per protocol.

**Scope & Constraints**  
Include: product thesis, first vertical, data sources, trust layer, business model.  
Exclude: political ideology, rage bait, broad legal commentary, generic AI startup language.

**Success Criteria**  
Passes if a reader can answer:

1. What is ELaB?
    
2. Who is it for?
    
3. Why is it different?
    
4. What must be built first?
    
5. What risk could kill it?
    

**Failure / Kill Conditions**  
Kill review if the piece becomes legal-news commentary, ideological argument, unsupported investment advice, or introduces process/framework terms into final prose. Protocol supports kill review for thesis drift, lock violations, framework contamination, and parallel writing.

**Authority Order**

1. Structure & logical flow
    
2. Claim integrity
    
3. Evidence & reasoning
    
4. Style & language  
    Matches protocol authority hierarchy.
    

**Round 1 Deliverables**  
Primary Author produces a flat structural draft with:

- opening thesis
    
- problem
    
- product definition
    
- wedge
    
- trust layer
    
- business model
    
- closing implication
    

No polish. No cleverness. No persuasion layer. Primary Author’s job is completeness, not refinement.

**Lock Statement**  
Round 0 locks the artifact as a strategic memo about ELaB as regulatory opportunity intelligence. Round 1 may draft only inside that frame.
