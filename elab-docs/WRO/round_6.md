## Final Artifact

---

### Thesis

ELaB converts government actions into auditable business opportunity maps for operators.

---

### Problem

Laws are published as text and process, not as executable decisions.  
This creates a dependency on interpretation before action is possible.

---

### Product Definition

Core outputs:

- plain-language summary
    
- affected industries
    
- agencies involved
    
- compliance requirements
    
- funding pathways (grants, contracts, loans)
    
- potential business models
    
- winners and losers
    

Standardized output per law:

- summary
    
- economic impact surface
    
- specific opportunity hypotheses
    
- minimum viable execution paths
    

Delivery layers:

- website (primary publishing layer)
    
- newsletter (distribution layer)
    

Each output must produce at least one executable action or be discarded.  
“Executable” requires a defined actor, action, and mechanism.

---

### Trust Layer

Primary failure modes include incorrect interpretation and unsupported inference.

Mitigation:

- source linking for every claim
    
- confidence scoring
    
- explicit uncertainty labeling
    
- “not legal advice” framing
    

Confidence scores reflect source strength and inference distance, not model certainty.

---

### Wedge (Initial Focus)

The system starts in one vertical with high regulatory density and visible capital flows to maximize signal and enforce depth.

---

### Business Model

- free: periodic opportunity digest (limited to 5 and redacted / summarized web post)
    
- paid: vertical-specific access on the website
    
- higher tiers: detailed opportunity breakdowns
    
- highest tier: full intelligence interface
    

Higher tiers increase specificity and reduce user-side interpretation effort.

---

### Core Differentiation

ELaB treats laws as economic events and produces directional outputs tied to economic consequences.

---

ELaB sits between government action and market response, removing the need to interpret source laws.
