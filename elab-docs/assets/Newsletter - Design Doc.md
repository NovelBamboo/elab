# ELaB Design Document

## System: **Policy-to-Market Intelligence Platform (PMI)**

_(derived from EO-14191 / education market restructuring)_

---

## 1. Premise (Locked)

A system that converts **live policy shifts (executive orders, legislation, funding reallocations)** into **actionable market intelligence** for operators (investors, builders, service providers) within **72 hours of policy emergence**.

Core claim:

> Policy is upstream of markets. Whoever operationalizes policy fastest captures asymmetric opportunity.

This system exists because **policy signals are currently readable but not operationalized at speed**.

---

## 2. System Boundary

### Included

- Federal + state policy ingestion (EOs, bills, grants)
    
- Economic impact modeling (sector-level projections)
    
- Beneficiary mapping (who wins/loses)
    
- Execution pathways (how to act)
    

### Excluded

- Political commentary
    
- Legal advice
    
- General news summarization
    

---

## 3. Core System Objects

### 3.1 Policy Object

- ID (EO-14191)
    
- Type (Executive Order / Bill / Grant)
    
- Funding Movement ($10–20B reallocation)
    
- Mechanism (voucher expansion, ESA, grant reprioritization)
    
- Time Horizon (immediate / phased)
    

---

### 3.2 Market Translation Layer

Transforms policy into:

- Sector impact (e.g. Private K-12, EdTech, Charter Ops)
    
- CAGR projections (e.g. EdTech compliance 25%)
    
- Capital flow direction
    
- Demand shifts
    

---

### 3.3 Opportunity Graph

Nodes:

- Entities (schools, EdTech firms, compliance vendors)
    
- Funding streams (grants, vouchers, ESAs)
    
- Regulatory constraints
    

Edges:

- “benefits from”
    
- “constrained by”
    
- “requires compliance with”
    

---

### 3.4 Execution Path Object

Concrete, testable actions:

Example:

- “Build ESA-compliant LMS with parent transparency features”
    
- “Partner with voucher-heavy states (AZ, FL, OH)”
    
- “Target compliance tooling for multi-state operators”
    

No vague recommendations allowed.

---

## 4. System Behavior (As-If Live)

### Trigger Event

New policy released (e.g. EO-14191)

### Within 72 Hours, System Produces:

1. **Policy Breakdown**
    
    - What changed structurally (not rhetorically)
        
2. **Capital Reallocation Map**
    
    - Where money moves
        
    - Who loses funding
        
3. **Market Expansion Model**
    
    - Sector growth projections
        
    - New TAM surfaces
        
4. **Beneficiary Ranking**
    
    - Tier 1: Direct winners (private schools, charter operators)
        
    - Tier 2: Infrastructure (ERP, compliance, LMS)
        
    - Tier 3: Indirect (real estate, workforce training)
        
5. **Execution Playbooks**
    
    - Investor actions
        
    - Builder opportunities
        
    - Operator strategies
        
6. **Risk Surface**
    
    - Legal constraints (e.g. Blaine Amendments)
        
    - Misallocation risks
        
    - Political reversibility
        

---

## 5. User Types (Concrete)

### 5.1 Operator (Primary)

- Runs or builds companies in affected sectors
    
- Needs: “What do I build next?”
    

### 5.2 Capital Allocator

- PE, VC, family office
    
- Needs: “Where does capital move before consensus?”
    

### 5.3 Policy Strategist

- Advocacy, think tanks
    
- Needs: “How does policy translate to real-world effects?”
    

---

## 6. Core Interface (Simulation)

### Dashboard Modules

1. **Policy Feed**
    
    - Ranked by economic impact (not recency)
        
2. **Impact Map**
    
    - Visual: funding flows → sectors
        
3. **Opportunity Stack**
    
    - Ranked execution plays (with constraints)
        
4. **Regulatory Friction Panel**
    
    - State-by-state variation
        
5. **Time Horizon Slider**
    
    - Immediate vs 2030 projections
        

---

## 7. Comparative Delivery Lineage

This system competes with:

### Policy Intelligence Platforms

- FiscalNote
    
- Quorum
    

→ Strength: policy tracking  
→ Failure: weak economic translation

---

### Financial Intelligence Platforms

- Bloomberg
    
- PitchBook
    

→ Strength: market data  
→ Failure: policy latency

---

### Gap (This System)

> No platform converts **policy → market → execution** in one pipeline.

---

## 8. Economic Engine

### Value Creation

- Speed advantage (early positioning)
    
- Interpretation advantage (less noise, more signal)
    

### Monetization

- Subscription (tiered by depth)
    
- High-ticket: execution playbooks + advisory
    

---

## 9. Constraints (Non-Negotiable)

- Policy interpretation must map to **observable funding shifts**
    
- Every opportunity must include:
    
    - dependency
        
    - risk
        
    - execution path
        
- No speculative narratives without economic grounding
    

---

## 10. Risks (System-Level)

### Structural

- Policy reversibility (administration change)
    
- Legal blocks (state constitutions)
    

### Market

- Overcrowding in obvious sectors
    
- Margin compression
    

### Operational

- State-by-state fragmentation
    
- Compliance complexity scaling
    

All explicitly present in source material.

---

## 11. Unknowns (Explicit)

- Exact contract allocation volumes (undefined in policy)
    
- Speed of state-level adoption
    
- Enforcement rigor on compliance
    

Unknowns are surfaced—not deferred. (AiD requirement)

---

## 12. Why This System Exists (Truth Layer)

Current reality:

- Policy analysis exists
    
- Market analysis exists
    
- Execution guidance exists
    

They are **disconnected**.

Result:

> Opportunity latency.

This system compresses:

> Policy → Understanding → Action
