## ANALYZE (What this step does)

User Journeys convert profiles into:  
👉 **observable product behavior**

They answer:

- When does the client show up?
    
- What do they do first?
    
- What must the system deliver to create a “win”?
    

This is critical because:

- Features come from journeys (not ideas)
    
- Poor journeys = bloated or misaligned product
    

---

## 🛤 User Journeys (All 3 Profiles)

---

# **🎤 1. Independent Operator / Founder Journey**

### **Stage 1: Opportunity Search**

- **Goal:** Find a viable business idea
    
- **Action:** Opens ELaB, browses recent laws or selects a vertical
    
- **System Response:** Displays laws with clear opportunity summaries
    

---

### **Stage 2: Opportunity Evaluation**

- **Goal:** Decide if an idea is worth pursuing
    
- **Action:** Clicks into a law breakdown
    
- **System Response:** Shows:
    
    - affected market
        
    - opportunity ideas
        
    - basic execution paths
        

---

### **Stage 3: Idea Selection**

- **Goal:** Choose one direction to act on
    
- **Action:** Compares 2–3 opportunities
    
- **System Response:** Highlights:
    
    - feasibility
        
    - required effort
        
    - potential upside
        

---

### **Stage 4: First Action**

- **Goal:** Start execution
    
- **Action:** Takes first step (research, outreach, build)
    
- **System Response:** Provides:
    
    - simple starting steps
        
    - key resources or signals to validate
        

---

👉 **Value Chain:**  
**Discover → Evaluate → Choose → Start**

👉 **Win:** Leaves with a **clear, actionable business idea tied to a law**

---

# **🎧 2. Small Fund / Independent Investor Journey**

### **Stage 1: Signal Monitoring**

- **Goal:** Track emerging opportunities
    
- **Action:** Reviews ELaB dashboard or newsletter
    
- **System Response:** Surfaces:
    
    - high-impact laws
        
    - early opportunity signals
        

---

### **Stage 2: Opportunity Analysis**

- **Goal:** Understand market implications
    
- **Action:** Opens a specific law
    
- **System Response:** Shows:
    
    - winners / losers
        
    - capital flows
        
    - sectors impacted
        

---

### **Stage 3: Investment Framing**

- **Goal:** Identify investable angles
    
- **Action:** Reviews opportunity hypotheses
    
- **System Response:** Provides:
    
    - business models
        
    - potential plays (equity, infra, services)
        

---

### **Stage 4: Decision / Positioning**

- **Goal:** Act on insight
    
- **Action:** Makes investment decision or tracks opportunity
    
- **System Response:** Reinforces:
    
    - confidence level
        
    - assumptions
        
    - risks
        

---

👉 **Value Chain:**  
**Track → Analyze → Frame → Act**

👉 **Win:** Identifies an **investable opportunity before market consensus**

---

# **🏢 3. Operator Inside Existing Business Journey**

### **Stage 1: Change Awareness**

- **Goal:** Understand what changed
    
- **Action:** Opens ELaB after regulatory update
    
- **System Response:** Provides:
    
    - simple explanation of the law
        
    - immediate relevance to business
        

---

### **Stage 2: Impact Assessment**

- **Goal:** Determine business impact
    
- **Action:** Reviews detailed breakdown
    
- **System Response:** Shows:
    
    - operational impact
        
    - compliance needs
        
    - risks and opportunities
        

---

### **Stage 3: Strategic Response**

- **Goal:** Decide what to do
    
- **Action:** Evaluates internal options
    
- **System Response:** Suggests:
    
    - strategic moves
        
    - areas to invest or adjust
        

---

### **Stage 4: Execution Alignment**

- **Goal:** Align team and act quickly
    
- **Action:** Shares insight internally
    
- **System Response:** Provides:
    
    - clear, shareable outputs
        
    - structured reasoning
        

---

👉 **Value Chain:**  
**Understand → Assess → Decide → Align**

👉 **Win:** Makes a **faster, clearer strategic decision than competitors**

## CHALLENGE (Where tension exists)

1. **Three journeys = three product modes**
    
    - Founder → exploration
        
    - Investor → signal intelligence
        
    - Operator → decision support
        

👉 Risk: feature overload if combined too early

---

2. **Different depth expectations**
    
    - Founder = lightweight
        
    - Investor = analytical
        
    - Operator = contextual
        

👉 One UI may not serve all well

---

3. **Timing differences**
    
    - Founder = anytime
        
    - Investor = continuous monitoring
        
    - Operator = reactive (triggered by change)
        

---

## TIGHTEN (1 Metric + 1 Decision)

**Metric:**  
➡️ Which journey reaches a “win” in the **fewest steps?**

**Decision:**  
➡️ Which journey defines the **MVP experience?**
